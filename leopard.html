Lets Visit Leopard

Leopards in Udawalawa

While Udawalawe is more famous for its elephant population, it is also home to leopards. The park's open grasslands and scrub areas create suitable conditions for leopards to thrive.

Leopards in hortain Plains

Located in the central highlands, Horton Plains is known for its unique ecosystems, and leopards have been reported in the park. The montane grasslands and cloud forests make it an interesting habitat for these elusive cats.

Leopards in Knuckles Mountain Range

The Knuckles Conservation Forest, part of the Knuckles Mountain Range, is another area where leopards have been spotted. This mountainous region provides a different type of habitat compared to the national parks.

Determinants of Deforestation

Deforestation in the rainforests of Sri Lanka is influenced by a multitude of factors. The encroachment of land for agricultural purposes, particularly for the establishment of tea and rubber plantations, presents a significant peril to the natural ecosystem. Furthermore, the detrimental practice of illegal logging, aimed at procuring valuable timber and fuelwood, serves to expedite the process of habitat destruction. Urbanisation and the implementation of infrastructure development projects can result in the fragmentation of habitats and the disturbance of wildlife corridors.

Human-Wildlife Conflict

As leopard habitats shrink, these big cats may come into contact with human settlements. This can result in conflicts, especially if leopards prey on domestic animals. Retaliatory killings by farmers protecting their livestock can occur, posing a direct threat to leopard populations.
