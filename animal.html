Indigenous animals in Sri Lanka

Sri Lankan Elephant (Elephas maximus maximus)

The Sri Lankan elephant is one of three recognized subspecies of the Asian elephant. Known for its smaller size compared to other Asian elephants, the Sri Lankan elephant is an iconic species and a cultural symbol in the country.

Sri Lankan Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus inornatus)

The sloth bear is the only bear species found in Sri Lanka. It inhabits various forest types and is recognizable by its shaggy coat. Conservation efforts are in place to protect the dwindling sloth bear population.

Hump-nosed Lizard (Lyriocephalus scutatus)

This endemic lizard species is known for its unique appearance, including a hump-like structure on its nose. It is found in the central hills and is a threatened species due to habitat loss.

Toque Macaque (Macaca sinica)

The toque macaque is another primate species endemic to Sri Lanka. It is recognizable by its distinctive crown of hair and is found in various forest types across the island.
