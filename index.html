Srilankan Wildlife

Sri Lanka, an Indian Ocean gem, beckons wildlife enthusiasts with its iconic Asian elephants in Udawalawe and Minneriya, leopards in Yala, and diverse birdlife in Bundala and Sinharaja. Coastal wonders like Mirissa and Kalpitiya offer thrilling whale watching. Beyond, scenic gems like Horton Plains and the Knuckles Range captivate. The cultural touch, highlighted by revered elephants, adds a unique charm. Sri Lanka: a compact haven for nature lovers.



Ocean Giants


The island is also considered the best place in the world to spot the largest animal that has ever lived on earth – the blue whale. The coastlines of Mirissa and Trincomalee offer the best locations for viewing these magnificent beasts while Kalpitiya is known as one of the world’s top ten best sites to catch a glimpse of the amazing sperm whale – the biggest toothed whale in the world.
Meanwhile, Mirissa additionally offers visitors the best chance at seeing both these magnificent mammals of the ocean on the same tour.

Bird Diversity


Sri Lanka is also an amazing place to visit for those interested in birding. It has dozens of bird sanctuaries and Important Bird Areas where an eye-catching array of avifauna can be viewed. The Kumana and Bundala National Parks are birding hotspots where almost every species of bird recorded in Sri Lanka can be seen. The Sinharaja Bird Wave is also a well-known attraction in Sri Lanka, providing visitors with the largest and longest studied bird waves on the island.

The wildlife of Sri Lanka is one of the country’s defining attractions and few countries can rival Sri Lankan wildlife and its combination of big game safari animals, species densities, and its tourism infrastructure. It is well and truly the ultimate safari island.

Safari Around


The Sri Lankan elephant, a cultural icon and a symbol of prosperity, thrives in the island's diverse landscapes. Protected in national parks like Udawalawe and Minneriya, these gentle giants roam freely. Visitors can witness their social interactions and playful behavior, creating an unforgettable experience. Beyond their wildlife significance, Sri Lankan elephants hold a special place in religious and cultural events, embodying a harmonious connection between the people and the natural world. Sri Lanka's commitment to elephant conservation ensures that these majestic creatures continue to enchant and inspire visitors from around the world.
