Maduru Oya

Maduru Oya National Park is one of the National Parks of Sri Lanka. It was designated as a National Park on 9th November 1983. It was established under the Mahaweli development project and also acts as a catchment of the Maduru Oya Reservoir. It is 58,849 hectares in content is located in the dry zone and 314 km from Colombo and works as a sanctuary for wildlife. The Maduru Oya National Park is famous for its Elephant habitat. Other than Elephants there are wild animals such as Leopards, Sloth Bears, Sambhur, Spotted and Barking Deer, Wild Boar and Wild Buffalo. The Toque Macaque, Purple-faced Langur, the nocturnal Slender Loris, Porcupine, Black Naped Hare, Indian Pangolin and many varieties of Squirrels are also found in the park. Other than the wild animals it is home for over 100 species birds such as Lesser Adjutant, Woolly necked stork, Openbill, painted stork, Racket tailed Drongo, Yellow-fronted barbet, Sri Lankajungle fowl and spur fowl. Other than that there is a group of Veddas the indigenous people of Sri Lanka lives within the boundaries of Maduru Oya National Park. The park is less crowded from tourist and you can enjoy the wild animals and their behaviours in a peaceful manner. There are few resorts in the area to spend the night. Actually Maduru Oya national park is a hidden gem amongst the tourist attractions of Sri Lanka. There are large herds of elephants roaming in the park tend to be hidden by the seven feet tall guinea grass. This is actually a splendid place to get up close and personal with wild Elephants. Other than the wild animals you will come across some ancient ruins that date back around 3000 years. There is discovery of ancient sluice dating prior to the 6th century BCE on the old breached earthen bund of the Maduru Oya. This park is on the list of the least- visited parks in Sri Lanka and you will find complete tranquility as you view its beautiful fauna and flora.
