Locations You Might Love

Maduru Oya

Maduru Oya National Park is one of the National Parks of Sri Lanka. It was designated as a National Park on 9th November 1983. It was established under the Mahaweli development project and also acts as a catchment of the Maduru Oya Reservoir. It is 58,849 hectares in content is located in the dry zone and 314 km from Colombo and works as a sanctuary for wildlife. The Maduru Oya National Park is famous for its Elephant habitat. Other than Elephants there are wild animals such as Leopards, Sloth Bears, Sambhur, Spotted and Barking Deer, Wild Boar and Wild Buffalo. The Toque Macaque, Purple-faced Langur, the nocturnal Slender Loris, Porcupine, Black Naped Hare, Indian Pangolin and many varieties of Squirrels are also found in the park. Other than the wild animals it is home for over 100 species birds such as Lesser Adjutant, Woolly necked stork, Openbill, painted stork, Racket tailed Drongo, Yellow-fronted barbet, Sri Lankajungle fowl and spur fowl. Other than that there is a group of Veddas the indigenous people of Sri Lanka lives within the boundaries of Maduru Oya National Park.

Wasgamuwa National Park

Nestled in the central province of Sri Lanka, Wasgamuwa National Park is a biodiverse haven that beckons wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Renowned for its annual Elephant Gathering, the park hosts one of the largest congregations of wild elephants during the dry season. Beyond elephants, the park boasts a rich variety of wildlife, including leopards, deer, and over 140 species of birds. Safari adventures guided by knowledgeable experts offer an immersive experience, unveiling the park's picturesque landscapes, meandering rivers, and lush plains. As a testament to its commitment to conservation, Wasgamuwa actively engages in wildlife protection initiatives and sustainable tourism practices. Visitors can expect a captivating blend of wildlife encounters and scenic serenity in this extraordinary natural sanctuary.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park in southeast Sri Lanka is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including a high density of leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and over 200 bird species. With diverse landscapes ranging from dense forests to open grasslands, the park offers prime wildlife viewing. Block 1 is especially known for its frequent leopard sightings. The park also houses the ancient Sithulpawwa Rock Temple, adding cultural significance. Guided safaris led by knowledgeable naturalists provide an immersive experience, highlighting the park's commitment to conservation.

Udawalawa National Park

Udawalawe National Park, in southern Sri Lanka, is renowned for its diverse wildlife, notably its robust elephant population. The park's open grasslands and Udawalawe Reservoir create an ideal habitat for various species, making it a hotspot for wildlife enthusiasts. The Elephant Transit Home contributes to vital elephant conservation efforts. Guided safaris offer an immersive experience, providing insights into the park's ecology and ongoing conservation initiatives. Overall, Udawalawe promises a captivating blend of natural beauty, diverse habitats, and wildlife conservation.

Wilpattuwa National Park

Wilpattu National Park, Sri Lanka's largest, is a haven of untamed wilderness on the northwest coast. Known as the "Land of Lakes," it features diverse ecosystems, including dense jungles and natural lakes, providing a habitat for leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and various bird species. Guided safaris offer an opportunity to witness these creatures in their natural environment. Beyond wildlife, Wilpattu holds historical and cultural significance with ancient ruins. The park actively participates in conservation efforts to sustain its unique biodiversity, making it a captivating destination for nature and heritage enthusiasts.

Hortain Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park, located in Sri Lanka's central highlands, is renowned for its scenic plateau, highlighted by the iconic "World's End" cliff offering stunning panoramic views. The park is a biodiversity hotspot, home to unique flora and fauna, including sambar deer and leopards. Baker's Falls adds to its charm, while challenging hiking trails provide an immersive experience. Conservation efforts focus on preserving the park's delicate ecosystems, making it a captivating destination for nature enthusiasts.
